Section answers natural questions such as: what is the book about, at whom it is aimed, and how to read it. Section defines a first model of games, which is the common denominator of (almost all) models studied in this book.

We introduce the computational models that we use in Section and briefly define linear programming. In Section we list the main objectives appearing in all chapters. A few notions will be useful throughout the book: they are developed in this chapter. We start with the notion of automata, discussed in Section, and then memory for strategies, in Section. We then show how automata and memory structures can be used to construct reductions between games in Section. We introduce in Section the notions of subgames and traps.

The notion of fixed point algorithms is central to the study of games. We first recall the main two methods for proving the existence and computing fixed points in Section. We then give an overview of two prominent families of fixed point algorithms for games: value iteration algorithms in Section and strategy improvement algorithms in Section.
